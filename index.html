CU Rover Team

CU ROVER TEAM

We are the CU Rover Team - a University of Colorado Boulder student organization - competing in the University Rover Challenge. We provide a free and collaborative environment to learn about, design, and contribute to space robotics. We are open to all students. No experience is required to join! 

JOIN US!

University Rover Challenge

The University Rover Challenge is a yearly competition held in Hanksville, Utah. The aim is to design a martian rover capable of assisting humans in a variety of tasks such as autonomously navigating harsh terrain, interacting with a lander, analyzing samples for life, and managing loads.

Our Subteams

Drive

Design of the basic drive system of the rover including the suspension, drive frame, wheels and embedded system with localization sensors.

learn more

Arm

Developing the robotic arm used by the rover for grasping items and manipulating with a lander during the missions.

learn more

Science

Searching for life in a sample using an auger and custom on-board lab to determine whether life is extinct, extant, or absent.

learn more

Communications

Creating long-distance communications between mission control and the rover as well as GPS localization.
